Why ppl fail to achieve in the workplace

Stress and time pressure:

When under pressure to make a quick decision, people may not thoroughly evaluate all options and make rushed choices. Lack of information or understanding: Not having enough knowledge about a situation or the potential consequences of a decision can lead to poor choices.

Poor communication:

Misunderstandings or lack of clarity in communication can result in incorrect assumptions and bad decisions. Personal agendas: Individuals might prioritize their own interests over the best interests of the company when making decisions.

Emotional influence:

Strong emotions like anger or excitement can cloud judgment and lead to impulsive decisions. Groupthink: When team members conform to the majority opinion without critically evaluating all options, leading to poor group decisions.

Lack of accountability:

When individuals don't feel responsible for the consequences of their decisions, they may be more likely to make poor choices.

Organizational culture:

A company culture that discourages dissent or critical thinking can contribute to poor decision-making.
